Q1 Streams of knowledge in ancient India

⚡ Core Intuition

Indian knowledge is a tree: roots = Vedas, branches = Upavedas (applied) + Vedangas (tools), leaves = arts, sciences, philosophy.

📖 Concept Explained
  • Vedas (4) — Rig, Yajur, Sama, Atharva — core revealed knowledge.
  • Upavedas (4) — applied sciences: Ayurveda, Dhanurveda, Gandharvaveda, Sthapatyaveda.
  • Vedangas (6) — auxiliary limbs: phonetics, ritual, grammar, etymology, metre, astronomy.
  • Upangas (4) — Purana, Nyaya, Mimamsa, Dharmashastra.
  • Darshanas (6) — orthodox philosophical schools.
  • Itihasa — Ramayana, Mahabharata (history-epics).
  • Agamas / Tantras — sectarian worship texts.
🔗 Connects to Vedas Upavedas Vedangas Darshanas
📐 Exam Answer Structure
  • Define "stream of knowledge" — organised body of vidya.
  • Draw tree diagram (Vedas → branches).
  • List 4 Vedas + 4 Upavedas + 6 Vedangas with 1-line each.
  • Mention Itihasa, Purana, Darshana, Agama.
  • State purpose: integrate spiritual + worldly knowledge.
💡 Example

Modern medicine traces to Ayurveda (Upaveda); modern grammar traces to Panini's Ashtadhyayi (Vyakarana Vedanga).

📌 "V-U-V-U-D" — Vedas, Upavedas, Vedangas, Upangas, Darshanas.

✅ Model Exam Answer
  1. Define stream of knowledge — organised body of vidya.
  2. Draw tree diagram (above).
  3. 4 Vedas + 4 Upavedas + 6 Vedangas + 4 Upangas + 6 Darshanas — list with 1-line role.
  4. Add Itihasa (epics) + Agamas (sectarian).
  5. Conclude — spiritual (para) + worldly (apara) integrated.

Q2 Classification of ancient Indian texts — Shruti vs Smriti

⚡ Core Intuition

Shruti = "what was heard" (revealed, eternal); Smriti = "what was remembered" (composed by sages, mutable).

📖 Concept Explained
  • Shruti — apaurusheya (not human-made), revealed.
  • Contains 4 Vedas + Brahmanas + Aranyakas + Upanishads.
  • Smriti — paurusheya (human-composed), tradition-based.
  • Contains Itihasa, Puranas, Dharmasastras, Agamas, Sutras.
  • Each Veda has 4 layers: Samhita (hymns) → Brahmana (ritual) → Aranyaka (forest) → Upanishad (philosophy).
  • Shruti is authoritative; Smriti is interpretive.
🔗 Connects to Vedas Upanishads Brahmanas Itihasa
📐 Exam Answer Structure
  • Define Shruti and Smriti (etymology + authority).
  • Draw hierarchy diagram (Shruti tree + Smriti tree).
  • List contents of each (4 layers of Veda; 5 smriti types).
  • Compare table (origin, authority, mutability).
Definition

Shruti = directly revealed to rishis. Smriti = composed from human memory of dharma.

💡 Example

Rigveda is Shruti (eternal hymns); Manusmriti is Smriti (Manu's law code).

📌 "SBAU" = 4 Veda-layers: Samhita — Brahmana — Aranyaka — Upanishad.

✅ Model Exam Answer
  1. Define Shruti (apaurusheya, revealed) and Smriti (paurusheya, composed).
  2. Draw tree (above).
  3. Shruti = 4 Vedas — 4 layers (Samhita, Brahmana, Aranyaka, Upanishad).
  4. Smriti = Itihasa, Purana, Dharmasastra, Sutra, Agama.
  5. Compare authority — Shruti supreme, Smriti subordinate.

Q3 Six Astika Darsanas — orthodox philosophy

⚡ Core Intuition

Six orthodox schools — each accepts Vedic authority and focuses on one slice of reality: logic, atoms, mind, meditation, ritual, ultimate knowledge.

📖 Concept Explained
  • Nyaya (Gautama) — logic, valid means of knowledge (pramana).
  • Vaisheshika (Kanada) — atomism, 7 categories of reality.
  • Samkhya (Kapila) — dualism: Purusha (consciousness) + Prakriti (matter).
  • Yoga (Patanjali) — 8-limbed mental discipline (ashtanga).
  • Mimamsa (Jaimini) — Vedic ritual exegesis, dharma.
  • Vedanta (Badarayana) — Upanishadic knowledge, Brahman.
🔗 Connects to Astika Vedas Moksha Pramana
📐 Exam Answer Structure
  • Define Astika — accepts Vedic authority.
  • Draw 6-hexagon diagram with focus tag.
  • For each: founder + central focus + 1-line.
  • Pair-wise alliance: Nyaya-Vaisheshika, Samkhya-Yoga, Mimamsa-Vedanta.
💡 Example

Patanjali's Yoga-Sutras (~200 BCE) systematise meditation; Sankara's Advaita Vedanta (~700 CE) interprets Upanishads.

📌 "NV-SY-MV" = Nyaya-Vaisheshika — Samkhya-Yoga — Mimamsa-Vedanta (3 allied pairs).

✅ Model Exam Answer
  1. Define Astika = accepts Veda authority.
  2. Draw 6-hexagon diagram (above).
  3. For each school: founder + key text + central focus (table).
  4. State pair-alliances (NV / SY / MV).
  5. Common goal: liberation (Moksha).

Q4 Nastika systems — Buddhism, Jainism, Carvaka

⚡ Core Intuition

Three heterodox schools reject Veda authority — Buddhism (no-self + 4 truths), Jainism (non-violence + 7 truths), Carvaka (only matter, no soul).

📖 Concept Explained
  • Buddhism (Gautama Buddha) — 4 Noble Truths, 8-fold path, anatman.
  • Jainism (Mahavira) — Ahimsa, anekantavada (many-sided truth), kaivalya.
  • Carvaka / Lokayata — materialism, only perception valid, no afterlife.
  • All three deny Vedas as ultimate authority.
  • Buddhism + Jainism accept karma/rebirth; Carvaka rejects both.
🔗 Connects to Nastika Ahimsa Karma Moksha
📐 Exam Answer Structure
  • Define Nastika — rejects Vedic authority.
  • Draw 3-column comparison.
  • Founder + core doctrine + key concept per school.
  • State differences: Carvaka most radical.
💡 Example

Buddhist Asoka spread non-violence; Jain monks sweep paths to avoid harming insects; Carvakans called ritual a "fraud of priests".

📌 "BJC" — Buddha (suffering), Jina (ahimsa), Carvaka (matter).

✅ Model Exam Answer
  1. Define Nastika = does not accept Veda.
  2. 3-column comparison diagram (above).
  3. Founder + central doctrine + goal per school.
  4. Carvaka uniquely rejects karma/rebirth too.
  5. Historical impact — Ashoka (Buddhism), Mahavira (Jainism).

Q5 Purushartha + fundamental principles (Dharma, Karma, Moksha)

⚡ Core Intuition

A complete human life pursues 4 aims (Purushartha) — duty (dharma) and pleasure (kama) balanced by wealth (artha) climaxing in liberation (moksha).

📖 Concept Explained
  • Dharma — right conduct, duty, cosmic order.
  • Artha — material wealth, livelihood.
  • Kama — desire, pleasure, art, love.
  • Moksha — liberation from samsara.
  • Karma — action + its result, governs cycle.
  • Samsara — cycle of birth-death-rebirth.
  • Ahimsa — non-violence in thought, word, deed.
  • First 3 = worldly (pravritti); Moksha = beyond (nivritti).
🔗 Connects to Dharma Karma Moksha Samsara Ahimsa
📐 Exam Answer Structure
  • Define Purushartha = 4 aims of human life.
  • Draw pyramid: Dharma → Artha → Kama → Moksha.
  • Define Karma, Samsara, Moksha with link.
  • Mention Ahimsa as universal ethic.
Karma — Samsara — Moksha

Karma = action-fruit; Samsara = its repeating cycle; Moksha = freedom from cycle.

💡 Example

A merchant earning honest wealth (artha) while donating (dharma) and raising family (kama) heads toward moksha in old age.

📌 "DAKM" — Dharma — Artha — Kama — Moksha (in priority order).

✅ Model Exam Answer
  1. Define Purushartha — 4 valid aims of human life.
  2. List each with 1-line + pyramid diagram.
  3. Define Karma, Samsara, Moksha — show cycle.
  4. Ahimsa as foundational ethic.
  5. Pravritti (worldly 3) vs Nivritti (moksha).

📚 Primary: IKS Unit 1 notes — principles section — IKS book Ch 1.

Q6 Six Vedangas — auxiliary disciplines

⚡ Core Intuition

Six "limbs" of Veda — tools to correctly pronounce, perform, parse, etymologise, metricate and time the Vedic texts.

📖 Concept Explained
  • Siksha — phonetics, pronunciation.
  • Kalpa — ritual procedure (sutras).
  • Vyakarana — grammar (Panini's Ashtadhyayi).
  • Nirukta — etymology (Yaska).
  • Chhanda — metre / prosody.
  • Jyotisha — astronomy, time-keeping for rituals.
🔗 Connects to Vedas Panini Sanskrit Astronomy
📐 Exam Answer Structure
  • Define Vedanga = limb of Veda.
  • List 6 with role + body-part analogy.
  • Draw 6-arm diagram (Veda as body).
💡 Example

Panini's Ashtadhyayi (Vyakarana, ~500 BCE) defines Sanskrit grammar in 3,959 sutras — earliest formal grammar in world.

📌 "SKVNCJ" — Siksha — Kalpa — Vyakarana — Nirukta — Chhanda — Jyotisha.

✅ Model Exam Answer
  1. Define Vedanga = "limb of Veda", auxiliary disciplines.
  2. List 6 with body-part metaphor.
  3. 1-line role + key text/author per Vedanga.
  4. Purpose — preserve and correctly use Vedas.

Q1 Ashtanga Yoga — the eightfold path of Patanjali

⚡ Core Intuition

Ashta (eight) + Anga (limbs). First 5 limbs prepare body + behaviour; last 3 prepare mind. Climb the ladder rung by rung — you cannot meditate on top of an unethical, restless life.

📖 Concept Explained
  • Sage Patanjali codified Yoga in Yoga-Sutra (~200 BCE).
  • "Ashtanga" = Sanskrit Ashta (8) + Anga (limb / part).
  • Limbs 1—2 = ethics (Yama + Niyama).
  • Limbs 3—4 = body (Asana + Pranayama).
  • Limb 5 = bridge (Pratyahara — withdraws senses).
  • Limbs 6—8 = mind (Dharana → Dhyana → Samadhi).
  • Goal: union of Atman (individual self) with Brahman (universal consciousness) → Moksha.
🔗 Connects to Patanjali Yoga-Sutra Yoga Darsana Samkhya Moksha
📐 Exam Answer Structure
  • Etymology — Ashta + Anga = 8 limbs, by Patanjali.
  • Draw 8-step staircase diagram (mandatory).
  • Define each limb in 1 line + Sanskrit term.
  • Group: ethics (1—2) — body (3—4) — bridge (5) — mind (6—8).
  • Psychological + spiritual significance — last paragraph.
💡 Example

A new student first practises Ahimsa (not hurting), then Saucha (cleanliness), then sits in steady Padmasana — only then can breath, mind, and meditation deepen.

Q2 The 5 Yamas and 5 Niyamas — ethical foundation of yoga

⚡ Core Intuition

Yama = "don't do this to others" (5 restraints). Niyama = "do this to yourself" (5 observances). Together they purify behaviour before meditation begins.

📖 Concept Explained
  • 5 Yamas — outward moral code (social ethics).
  • 5 Niyamas — inward discipline (personal ethics).
  • Both = limbs 1 & 2 of Ashtanga, the unshakable base.
  • Without these, higher limbs collapse — like building on sand.

Q3 Rasa Siddhanta — the nine Rasas of Bharata's Natyasastra

⚡ Core Intuition

Rasa = aesthetic "flavour" the audience tastes when art is performed. Bharata Muni's recipe: Vibhava (cause) + Anubhava (reaction) + Vyabhicari Bhava (transient feelings) → evokes Sthayi Bhava (permanent emotion) → audience experiences Rasa.

📖 Concept Explained
  • Natyasastra by Bharata Muni (~200 BCE) — earliest treatise on performing arts.
  • Bharata listed 8 Rasas; Abhinavagupta later added Shanta = 9 Rasas.
  • Famous sutra: "vibhavanubhava-vyabhicari-samyogad rasa-nishpattih".
  • Each Rasa has a unique Sthayi Bhava (permanent emotional state).
  • Rasa converts ordinary emotion into universal aesthetic experience (Rasanubhava).
🔗 Connects to Bharata Muni Natyasastra Abhinavagupta Aesthetics
📐 Exam Answer Structure
  • Define Rasa + cite Bharata's formula.
  • Draw 9-Rasa wheel (mandatory).
  • Table — Rasa — Sthayi Bhava — trigger.
  • Explain 4 supporting elements (Vibhava, Anubhava, Sthayi, Vyabhicari).
  • Significance — universal emotion, Indian aesthetics.

🧠 Rasa-formula (Bharata)

Vibhava + Anubhava + Vyabhicari Bhava
            →  (combine)
    Sthayi Bhava (permanent emotion)
            →  (aesthetic sublimation)
          RASA  (audience experience)
Definition — 4 supporting elements
  • Vibhava — cause / stimulus of emotion (Alambana = person, Uddipana = environment).
  • Anubhava — physical/verbal reaction expressing the emotion.
  • Sthayi Bhava — permanent dominant emotion (8/9 of them, one per Rasa).
  • Vyabhicari (Sancari) Bhava — transient emotions like anxiety, joy that flicker around the main emotion.
💡 Example

Watching Rama's exile: Sita weeping (Anubhava) + forest hardship (Vibhava) + fleeting hope/despair (Vyabhicari) → Soka (Sthayi Bhava) → audience tastes Karuna Rasa.

  2. Cite Bharata's sutra: vibhava + anubhava + vyabhicari → Rasa.
  3. Draw 9-Rasa wheel.
  4. Table of 9 Rasas with Sthayi Bhavas.
  5. 4 supporting elements explained.
  6. Abhinavagupta added Santa = 9th.
  7. Significance — foundation of Indian drama, dance, poetry; universal emotional experience.

Q5 Panini's contribution to linguistics — the Ashtadhyayi

⚡ Core Intuition

Panini (~5th century BCE) wrote a grammar so compact and rule-driven that Chomsky calls it a precursor to generative grammar. ~4000 sutras in 8 chapters — language as algebra.

📖 Concept Explained
  • Ashtadhyayi = "Eight Chapters" (~3959 sutras) — exhaustive grammar of Sanskrit.
  • Each chapter is split into 4 padas (subsections).
  • Uses meta-language — symbols, abbreviations (anubandhas), pratyaharas.
  • Siva Sutras — 14 phonetic strings ordering all Sanskrit sounds.
  • Karaka theory — 6 semantic roles (karta, karma, karana, sampradana, apadana, adhikarana) — anticipates case theory.
  • Dhatu + Pratyaya — verbal roots + suffixes generate all words.
  • Influenced modern NLP, formal grammars, computer-language design.
🔗 Connects to Vyakarana Vedanga Patanjali Mahabhashya Chomsky NLP
📐 Exam Answer Structure
  • Introduce Panini + Ashtadhyayi (~5th cent BCE, 8 chapters, ~4000 sutras).
  • Draw 8-chapter block diagram.
  • List 5 features — rule-based, generative, economy, meta-rules, Siva-sutras.
  • Mention Karaka, Dhatu-Pratyaya, Sandhi rules.
  • Modern influence — generative grammar, NLP.

Q6 Vakyasastra & Pramanasastra — sentence-science & knowledge-science

⚡ Core Intuition

Vakyasastra asks "how does a sentence convey meaning?" — syntax & semantics. Pramanasastra asks "when is knowledge valid?" — epistemology, with Sabda (verbal testimony) as the linguistic bridge.

📖 Concept Explained
  • Vakyasastra — study of Vakya (sentence) — structure, meaning, discourse.
  • Sentences: declarative, imperative, interrogative.
  • Sphota theory (Bhartrihari, Vakyapadiya) — meaning bursts forth as a whole, not from words individually.
  • Anvaya (syntax) + Vyatireka (contrast) — syntactic relations.
  • Lakshana (secondary meaning) + Vyanjana (suggestive meaning) — pragmatics.
  • Pramanasastra — 6 valid sources of knowledge:
    • Pratyaksha — perception
    • Anumana — inference
    • Upamana — comparison
    • Arthapatti — postulation
    • Anupalabdhi — non-perception
    • Sabda — verbal testimony (links to language)
  • Sabda-bodha — process by which a hearer understands meaning from word-order + case-endings.
🔗 Connects to Bhartrihari Sphota Nyaya Mimamsa Kumarila
📐 Exam Answer Structure
  • Define both sastras separately.
  • Vakyasastra — sentence, Sphota, syntax-meaning, pragmatics.
  • Pramanasastra — 6 pramanas, Sabda focus, Sabda-bodha.
  • Table: Indian concept → modern equivalent.
  • Significance — early Indian semantics + epistemology.

Q7 Shadgunya — six-fold foreign policy

⚡ Core Intuition

Six standard moves a king can make toward another state. Pick by relative strength + circumstance.

📖 Concept Explained
  • Sandhi — peace treaty (when weaker).
  • Vigraha — open war (when stronger).
  • Yana — military march / preparation.
  • Asana — neutrality / staying still.
  • Samsraya — seek protection of a stronger king.
  • Dvaidhibhava — double policy: peace with one, war with another.
  • Choice depends on power ratio (sakti) + ground (desa) + time (kala).
🔗 Connects to Mandala Arthasastra Bk 7 Foreign policy

Q8 Financial management — Kosa & taxation

⚡ Core Intuition

Treasury is lifeblood. Tax like a bee — sip nectar without harming the flower.

📖 Concept Explained
  • Kosa — 5th limb of the state; the state treasury.
  • "No king without treasury, no treasury without people." — Kautilya.
  • Sources of revenue — agriculture (sita), mines (khani), forests (vana), commerce (vanik-patha), tolls (sulka).
  • Bhaga — 1/6 share of produce (standard tax rate).
  • Tax principles — moderate, predictable, fair, productive use.
  • Bee analogy — collect taxes like a bee collects nectar — without destroying the flower.
  • Adhyakshas — 30+ superintendents audit accounts (mint, gold, port, weights, agriculture).
  • Ahimsa-based wealth — gain by dharma (subha-labha), not exploitation.
